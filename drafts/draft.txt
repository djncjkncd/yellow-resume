4. The elevator pitch
Beyond a technical tool, SolidHour replaces manual processes with objective clarity, transforming administrative constraints into a performance asset that strengthens trust between the company and its employees.


5. The problem
Manual time management costs companies up to 20 hours monthly, creating financial risks and internal tensions due to unreliable data. SolldHour bridges this technological divide with an accessible, automated alternative that ensures data accuracy and operational perfomance.


6. Target
The application is aimed at companies wishing to free themselves from paper-based logs through a professional solution, without the costs or complexity of software for large groups.


8. Core features
Company perimeter (geofencing): definition of a virtual perimeter limiting clock-in activation to physical presence on-site, ensuring that tracking stops strictly at the company's boundaries.

Contextual notifications: automatic reminders are activated within the virtual perimeter, as well as when approaching shift limits, designed to assist the user and prevent forgetfulness.

Gamification: a streak system valuing consecutive on-time arrivals.

Real-time dashboard: an intuitive interface guaranteeing each employee perfect visibility of their recorded hourly activity.

Summary generator for interviews: automation of a summary report on the employee's full clock-in history for annual reviews.


9. Secondary features
Graphic visualization (infodata): a streamlined graphic restoration that converts raw individual data flows into clear trends and performance scores, offering management an immediate reading of work dynamics to lead the organization with objectivity and transparency.

"Master-Slave" offline protocol: a backup system allowing clock-ins without a network via a temporary QR code generated by a time synchronization algorithm between the employee and the "master" phone, with automatic synchronization upon the return of an internet connection.

Digitization of supporting documents: an integrated module allowing users to photograph and directly attach supporting documents for lateness or absence.

Automated insights generation: production of intelligent textual comments (employee and administrator accounts) that translate attendance history into constructive information or advice aligned with pre-configured company objectives.

Multilingual support: interface accessible in several languages.


10. Primary user journey
Team leader and collaborators within an organization: a structure wishing to modernize its attendance tracking. Environment with limited or intermittent connectivity. Transition from the paper attendance log to a digital solution.

1. Arrival at the workplace and automatic detection: The application's geofencing system detects entry into the organization's virtual perimeter and automatically triggers a contextual notification on each collaborator's smartphone. No prior manipulation is necessary: the alert serves as a reminder to confirm presence.

2. Activation of clock-in in offline mode: In the event of an unstable network connection or a power outage, the manager's phone, designated as the master device, generates a secure temporary QR code via the internal algorithm. The collaborator scans this code from their own terminal to validate their presence without internet. Data is stored locally and synchronized as soon as connectivity returns.

3. Justification of absence or lateness: A collaborator arrives with a delay due to an administrative or personal unforeseen event. Directly from the application, they photograph their supporting document via the integrated scanning module. The document is automatically linked to the day's session, guaranteeing full traceability without additional administrative steps.

4. Real-time tracking on the dashboard: Once the connection is restored, the administrator's interface updates. They can consult at a glance the summary of the team's hours over the week and any overtime. The graphic visualization allows for analyzing attendance dynamics without any manual compilation.

5. Report generation: At the end of the month, the manager generates, in a few seconds, a complete report of the hours worked. This exportable and auditable document can be sent directly to the accounting or HR department, thus replacing handwritten attendance sheets and eliminating entry errors.


11. Competitors : Uptimise; Jibble; Homebase; Skello


12.  Competitive edge :
Complexity and raw data: The complexity of implementing current tools hinders the digital transition of traditional structures. Furthermore, they often limit themselves to extracting raw data without offering an intelligent synthesis to facilitate decision-making.

Technical and functional limits: Some tools prioritize schedule management at the expense of an in-depth trend analysis. The absence of secure offline protocols also makes clocking impossible in areas with low connectivity, penalizing mobile sectors.

Costs and lack of automation: High prices often make these platforms inaccessible to small organizations. Moreover, their scope remains purely administrative, lacking automated tools to prepare performance interviews based on clocking history.
